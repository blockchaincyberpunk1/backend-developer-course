Contribute to an Open-Source Project

Course: Backend Developer Course

Assignment Deadline: [Insert Deadline]

Assignment Overview:
In this assignment, you will focus on contributing to an open-source project. The objective is to get involved in an open-source project that interests you or aligns with your skills, such as on platforms like GitHub. You will make a meaningful contribution to the project, such as fixing a bug, adding a feature, or improving documentation. Additionally, you will provide evidence of your contribution.

Instructions:
Please complete the following tasks to learn and practice contributing to open-source projects:

Task 1: Open-Source Project Selection (20 points)
Research and select an open-source project that interests you or aligns with your skills as a backend developer. Explain why you chose this particular project and mention any specific areas or issues you plan to contribute to.

Task 2: Contribution Planning (20 points)
Identify a meaningful contribution you can make to the selected open-source project. This contribution could include:

Fixing a bug or addressing an issue reported by the project community.
Adding a new feature or enhancing an existing one.
Improving project documentation to make it more accessible.
Task 3: Contribution Execution (40 points)
Make your planned contribution to the open-source project. Follow the project's contribution guidelines, code of conduct, and development process. Ensure that your contribution aligns with the project's goals and standards.

Task 4: Evidence of Contribution (10 points)
Provide evidence of your contribution, which may include:

A link to the pull request (PR) or merge request (MR) you submitted.
Screenshots or code snippets demonstrating the changes you made.
Communication with project maintainers or community members.
Task 5: Documentation (10 points)
Organize your assignment into a clear and well-structured document. Include explanations, details about the open-source project selected, your contribution, and evidence of your contribution. Ensure that your assignment is free of grammatical and spelling errors.

Submission Guidelines:

Compile your assignment into a document (PDF or Word).
Include any relevant references or sources you consulted during your research.
Grading Rubric:
Your assignment will be graded based on the following criteria:

Quality and appropriateness of the open-source project selection (20 points)
Thoughtful planning and execution of the contribution (20 points)
Effectiveness of the contribution to the project (40 points)
Clear evidence of the contribution (10 points)
Organization and presentation of the assignment (10 points)
